About

From the flight line to AI systems

A decade of keeping jet engines mission-ready taught me how to work inside systems where the cost of a mistake is real, and where you verify, you don't assume. That's the same discipline I'm now applying to AI evaluation and autonomous systems.

I'm an active-duty Aerospace Propulsion Craftsman in the U.S. Air Force, with more than ten years of hands-on experience maintaining and troubleshooting aircraft engines. That work is precise, procedural, and unforgiving of shortcuts — every inspection, every torque value, every signoff matters because a system has to perform under real conditions, not just on paper.

I'm now pursuing a BS in Applied Computing with an AI focus at the University of Arizona, working toward a transition into robotics, autonomous systems, and AI evaluation and testing. The throughline isn't a hard pivot — it's the same instinct for rigor, applied to a new kind of system.
